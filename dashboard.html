How to read Ease scores
Higher ease = easier to build and launch.
A 9 or 10 means you can execute with existing skills and minimal technical overhead in days to weeks. A 4 or 5 means significant DSP engineering, two-sided marketplace dynamics, or specialized co-founder requirements. Ease is scored across four factors:
Technical complexity
How hard is the core build? Pure SaaS/content = easy. DSP audio engineering or marketplace cold-start = hard.
Time to first revenue
Days to weeks = high ease. Months of pre-revenue build = low ease. Can you charge on day one?
Skill match
How well does your analytics, AI, and marketing background cover the requirements? Gaps need hires.
Dependencies
Does it require a co-founder, legal clearance, or key partnerships before launch? Each = lower ease.
Quick reference: 9–10 = launch solo this month · 7–8 = launch in 4–6 weeks with Claude Code · 5–6 = needs 1 technical hire or co-founder · 3–4 = 6+ month build, specialized engineering required

Their only growth levers are owned channels (email, SMS, loyalty, SEO) and AI visibility — but no fractional CMO offers compliance-first strategy, and no audit scores their AI citation risk including hallucination liability.
Unique moat
AI hallucination liability scoring — checking whether AI models cite the brand in unlicensed states — is a zero-competition intelligence product. No agency, tool, or consultant currently offers this. It addresses both the marketing team and the legal/compliance team, opening two budget sources per client.
Revenue per client
$499 audit (slightly above DTC, justified by compliance complexity) → $2,500/month retainer for MSOs → state compliance alert newsletter at $47/month (scales passively). One MSO retainer at $2,500 = equivalent revenue to 5 DTC retainers at lower delivery complexity because the strategy is systematic.
Key numbers
Meta, Google, TikTok advertising blocked in most US states for this category. Email and SMS are primary revenue channels — brands using AI-augmented retention see 22% higher repeat purchase rates. Weedmaps and Leafly are the primary LLM trust signals — review velocity there directly influences AI recommendation share.

Model Assumptions
Audit ramp
Q1: 1.5/week avg · Q2: 3/week · Q3–Q4: 5/week. Year 2: 8/week. Year 3: 12/week. Year 5: 20/week. Year 10: 30/week. Each audit requires ~30 min QA at early stage — automation via n8n required at 15+/week.
Retainer conversion
Target: 15–20% of audit clients convert to retainer within 90 days. First retainer by Month 5. 3 retainers by Q4 Y1. 8 by Y2. 35 by Y5. AI cost per retainer client: $0.04–$0.10/month.
Index growth
Starts Q3 Y1 with 20 brands. 50 by Q4. 100 by Y2. 250 by Y3. 1,000 by Y5. 3,000 by Y10. At $147/month/brand, index revenue overtakes audit revenue by Y3. This is the acquisition asset.
AI token cost
$0.09/audit (Sonnet 4.6 standard). $0.041/audit (cache+batch optimized). Index: $45/month for 500 brands via Batch API. Carousel Check: $0.003/check (Haiku 4.5). Tokens are NOT the margin risk.
