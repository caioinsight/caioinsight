geo GEO & Visibility · 13 terms
GEO — Generative Engine Optimization
Getting AI to recommend your brand — like SEO but for ChatGPT
GEO
Simple version
GEO is the work of getting AI assistants like ChatGPT to mention and recommend your brand. Think of it like regular SEO — except instead of trying to show up on Google's list of blue links, you are trying to get included in the AI's actual answer when someone asks a question like 'what's the best protein powder?'
Full definition
Generative Engine Optimization is the practice of structuring and distributing brand content so that AI search engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Claude — retrieve and cite it when answering user queries. Unlike traditional SEO which targets keyword ranking algorithms, GEO targets the retrieval and synthesis layer of AI systems. Over 60% of all search interactions now involve an AI-generated component.
Why it matters for your brand
This is the entire reason CAIO Insight exists. Brands that are invisible in AI-generated answers are losing purchase decisions they never even know are happening.
Vector / Coordinate Space
The invisible map where AI places every brand — yours needs to be in the right spot
GEO
Simple version
Imagine AI has a giant invisible map. Every brand, product, and idea has a spot on that map. When someone asks 'what's the best moisturizer?' — the AI looks at where that question falls on the map, then picks brands whose spots are closest to it. If your brand is on the wrong part of the map, or not on the map at all, the AI skips you completely.
Full definition
AI language models represent every word, concept, and brand as a mathematical vector — a set of numbers defining its position in a high-dimensional space. When a user submits a query, the model maps it to a coordinate and retrieves content whose vector position is most proximate. Brands whose data falls outside the relevant coordinate clusters are systematically omitted from synthesized answers, regardless of ad spend or Google rank.
Why it matters for your brand
The Vector Alignment Score in your CAIO brief measures exactly how close your brand's position is to the questions your customers are asking AI. A low score means you are in the wrong neighborhood on the map.
Synthesis / Zero-Click
AI reads 10 sources and writes one answer — if your brand is not in it, the shopper never finds you
GEO
Simple version
Old Google showed you a list of links. You picked one and clicked. Now AI reads a bunch of sources and writes one combined answer — no clicking needed. That is zero-click. Synthesis is the AI's process of combining multiple sources into that single answer. Traditional search ran on discovery. Agentic search runs on verification.
Full definition
Synthesis is the process by which a generative AI model combines retrieved information from multiple sources into a single coherent response. Zero-click describes the user experience of receiving a complete answer without visiting any external website. As synthesis replaces traditional search, brand visibility no longer requires a click — it requires being included in the generated output.
AI Hallucination / Hallucination Liability
When AI confidently makes up facts about your brand — a legal risk for regulated businesses
GEO
Simple version
Sometimes AI confidently states things that are not true. If AI tells a customer your product ships to all 50 states when it only ships to 12, that is a hallucination. For brands in regulated industries, this is dangerous — AI could recommend your product in a state where you are not legally allowed to sell it. That is hallucination liability.
Full definition
An AI hallucination occurs when a language model generates factually incorrect output with high confidence. Hallucination liability is the legal and reputational risk arising when an AI model incorrectly cites a brand in markets or geographies where it has no license or product availability. A unique and unaddressed risk for compliance-constrained verticals.
Why it matters for your brand
CAIO is the only diagnostic that checks for hallucination liability — auditing whether AI models are incorrectly recommending your brand in markets where you are not licensed or available. No other audit tool in this segment does this.
Compliance Friction
AI safety rules quietly pushing your brand out of results without telling you
GEO
Simple version
AI has safety rules built in. If your brand uses certain words or operates in certain industries, those safety rules might quietly decide to stop recommending you — even when everything you sell is perfectly legal. Compliance friction is how much those invisible safety filters are working against your brand. The scary part is it happens silently.
Full definition
Compliance friction measures the degree to which a model's safety and alignment guardrails suppress a brand's data during synthesis. Brands in regulated categories face systematic suppression when their content contains unstructured compliance signals — unqualified health claims, age-restricted language, or legally ambiguous copy — that trigger conservative retrieval filters.
Why it matters for your brand
Signal 3 in the CAIO four-layer diagnostic. High friction means your brand can have perfect schema, strong reviews, and an llms.txt file — and still be suppressed. Mapping the friction load shows exactly which content is triggering safety filters and how to restructure it.
Compliance-Constrained Vertical
Industries where most advertising is blocked — making AI visibility even more critical
GEO
Simple version
Some industries — like cannabis, hemp, certain supplements, and some financial products — are not allowed to advertise on Meta, Google, or TikTok. That means they cannot reach customers the normal way. For these brands, AI visibility is not just helpful — it is one of the only growth levers available. Getting recommended by ChatGPT is the equivalent of a TV ad they cannot run.
Full definition
Compliance-constrained verticals are industry categories operating under regulatory restrictions that limit access to major paid advertising platforms. Brands in these categories must rely heavily on owned channels (email, SMS, loyalty) and organic discovery — including AI citations. CAIO uses this term publicly rather than naming specific restricted categories.
Review Velocity / Trust Signal
How fast you are getting new reviews — and why AI uses it as a credibility check
GEO
Simple version
AI uses reviews like proof that a brand is real and trustworthy. Review velocity is how fast and often you are getting new reviews. A brand with 500 reviews all posted four years ago looks outdated to AI. A brand with 25 new reviews this month looks active and credible. More recent reviews = higher credibility in AI's eyes.
Full definition
Review velocity measures the recency and frequency of new customer reviews across trusted platforms — Google, Amazon, Trustpilot, platform-specific sites. Trust signals are data points AI models use to assess entity credibility during retrieval, including review volume, recency, star distribution, and cross-platform consistency.
Agentic Commerce / Agentic Storefront
AI that shops for you — and the store setup required to let it
GEO
Simple version
Imagine telling ChatGPT 'order me the best running shoes under $100' and it finds the shoes, adds them to a cart, and checks out for you — without you touching a website. That is agentic commerce. As of May 2026, fewer than 30 Shopify merchants have activated this. The window to build it first is wide open.
Full definition
Agentic commerce refers to AI agents autonomously performing shopping tasks — browsing, evaluating, selecting, and completing purchases — on behalf of users without manual human interaction. Agentic storefronts are e-commerce implementations with structured product data, accessible checkout flows, and API-compatible product feeds that allow AI agents to navigate and transact.
Agentic Search / Non-Branded Query
When someone asks AI 'what is the best X' without already knowing your brand
GEO
Simple version
Agentic search is when AI does the searching on your behalf instead of you typing into Google. A non-branded query is a search that does not use a brand name — like 'best shampoo for curly hair' instead of 'Brand X shampoo.' These are the most important queries for discovery. If AI recommends you in response to a non-branded query, you just reached someone who never knew you existed.
Full definition
Agentic search describes AI-driven information retrieval where an agent proactively searches on behalf of a user goal. Non-branded queries are intent-driven queries that do not contain a specific brand name. These queries represent the highest-value GEO opportunity because they reach customers at the discovery stage with no prior brand preference.
Citation Velocity
How often and how recently AI has been mentioning your brand
GEO
Simple version
Citation velocity tracks how frequently and recently AI platforms have been mentioning your brand in their answers. A brand with high citation velocity is being recommended often and recently. A brand with low velocity is rarely mentioned — or was mentioned a lot once but has faded. Velocity matters because AI systems weight recent mentions more heavily.
Full definition
Citation velocity is a GEO metric measuring the frequency and recency of brand mentions across AI platform responses over a rolling time window. Tracked through multi-run query sampling across platforms. Brands with rising citation velocity are gaining coordinate alignment. Brands with declining velocity are drifting outside relevant training clusters or being displaced by competitors.
Multi-Dimensional
Why AI understands brands in many ways at once — not just one simple score
GEO
Simple version
When you describe a friend to someone, you do not use just one word — you say they are funny, tall, from Chicago, and loves cooking. AI does the same with brands, understanding them across hundreds of qualities at once. That is multi-dimensional. A brand positioned well on many dimensions is far more likely to be cited than a brand that only stands out on one thing.
Full definition
Multi-dimensional refers to the nature of vector space representations in LLMs, where each entity is encoded across thousands of dimensions simultaneously — price range, category, use case, tone, audience, geography, and hundreds more. Comprehensive, consistent brand content across all touchpoints outperforms narrow single-channel optimization because it builds position across more dimensions.
Signal 0 & Signal 6
The pre-flight Cloudflare check and the ChatGPT carousel inclusion test
GEO
Simple version
Signal 0 is the very first check in every CAIO audit — before anything else. It checks whether Cloudflare security is accidentally blocking AI search robots from even reaching your website. If they are blocked, nothing else matters. Signal 6 tests whether your brand appears in ChatGPT's product carousel, which gives 3× the visibility of a regular text mention. Signal 6 is what the free Carousel Readiness Check tool tests for.
Full definition
The CAIO diagnostic runs seven signals. Signal 0 (pre-flight) verifies that AI search crawlers — OAI-SearchBot, PerplexityBot, Claude-SearchBot — are not blocked at the infrastructure layer by Cloudflare Bot Fight Mode or robots.txt. Signal 6 (carousel inclusion) tests whether a brand's structured data and Google Shopping feed meet requirements for ChatGPT product carousel inclusion. 83% of carousel products source from Google Shopping organic.
ai AI & LLMs · 9 terms
LLM — Large Language Model
The brain behind ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini — learned from reading the internet
AI
Simple version
An LLM is the AI brain behind assistants like ChatGPT and Claude. It learned how to understand and write language by reading a truly enormous amount of text — basically most of the internet, millions of books, and countless articles. Now it can understand questions and write answers that sound like a human wrote them. When someone asks 'what moisturizer should I buy?' — an LLM generates the recommendation.
Full definition
A Large Language Model is a deep learning system trained on massive text corpora to perform natural language understanding and generation. LLMs use transformer architecture with billions of parameters to predict probable token sequences given an input context. Major commercial LLMs: OpenAI GPT (ChatGPT), Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini, Meta LLaMA, Microsoft Copilot.
Why it matters for your brand
These are the systems routing purchase decisions in your category right now. If they cannot find your brand, your brand does not exist in the AI-mediated shopping experience.
RAG — Retrieval-Augmented Generation
AI searches the web first, then writes the answer — you need to be found in the search step
AI
Simple version
When you ask AI a question, it does two things. First it searches for relevant web pages (retrieval). Then it reads those pages and writes an answer using them (generation). RAG is the name for that two-step process. If your brand is not found in the first step, it cannot be included in the answer written in the second step.
Full definition
Retrieval-Augmented Generation is a technique where an AI system queries an external knowledge base or live web index to retrieve relevant context before generating a response. Retrieved documents are injected into the model's context window. RAG retrieval probability — Signal 2 in the CAIO diagnostic — measures the likelihood that a brand's pages will be retrieved, which is a prerequisite for appearing in the generated answer.
Why it matters for your brand
Signal 2 in the four-layer diagnostic. A score of zero means you are never retrieved, regardless of what the AI knows about you from its training data.
Token / Context Window
How AI measures and bills for text — roughly three-quarters of a word per token
AI
Simple version
AI reads and counts text in small chunks called tokens. One token is roughly three-quarters of a word. So 'hello world' is about 3 tokens. The context window is how much text AI can hold in its working memory at one time. AI API pricing is based on how many tokens are sent and received.
Full definition
A token is the base unit of text processed by an LLM. API pricing is split between input tokens (text sent to the model) and output tokens (text the model generates), with output costing 5× more than input. A full CAIO diagnostic audit costs approximately $0.09 in tokens at Sonnet 4.6 pricing — making token cost negligible relative to the $449 price.
Training Data / Training Cluster
Everything AI read to learn — if your brand was not there, AI barely knows you
AI
Simple version
AI learned everything it knows by reading text — webpages, books, articles, and forum posts. All that reading material is called training data. A training cluster is a neighborhood on AI's invisible map where related brands and ideas live because they were often mentioned together. If your brand was barely mentioned, AI does not really know who you are.
Full definition
Training data is the text corpus used to train an LLM's base model. Training clusters are emergent groupings of semantically related entities within the model's parameter space. Brands that co-occur frequently in training data end up positioned near each other in vector space. Wikipedia is the single strongest predictor of LLM entity recognition across all major platforms.
Semantic / Semantic Search
AI understands what you mean, not just the exact words you typed
AI
Simple version
Old-style search matched exact words. If you typed 'good face wash,' it only found pages with those exact words. Semantic search understands meaning. Type 'something gentle for dry irritated skin' and AI understands you probably want a mild moisturizing cleanser — even though you never used those words. This is why keyword stuffing does not work for GEO.
Full definition
Semantic search retrieves results based on meaning and intent rather than keyword pattern matching. AI models encode queries and content as vectors in a shared embedding space, retrieving content with high cosine similarity to the query vector — surfacing conceptually related content regardless of exact wording.
Entity / Entity Recognition
Whether AI recognizes your brand as a real known thing — not just words on a page
AI
Simple version
An entity is anything AI recognizes as a real, distinct thing — a brand, person, place, or product. Entity recognition is AI confirming 'yes, I know what this is.' If AI does not recognize your brand as a known entity, it treats you like a stranger — and tends not to recommend strangers. Strong entity recognition comes from consistent branding, Wikipedia presence, and cross-platform mentions.
Full definition
In AI systems, an entity is a discrete named object with a stable identity. Entity recognition is a model's ability to resolve a mention to a known entity in its knowledge base. Strong entity signals include Wikipedia presence, Schema.org Organization markup with sameAs properties, cross-platform mentions in credible sources, and consistent brand name usage. Wikipedia has a 0.810 correlation with LLM visibility — the single strongest predictor found in published GEO research.
Prompt / System Prompt
The question you type to AI, and the hidden instructions a developer sets in advance
AI
Simple version
A prompt is the question or instruction you type to AI — like 'what is the best protein powder?' A system prompt is a set of hidden instructions a developer writes before you ever start the conversation — like briefing a customer service rep on how to behave before their first call. In the CAIO audit, the system prompt contains the four-layer methodology and scoring rubric.
Full definition
A prompt is any text input submitted to an LLM to generate a response. A system prompt is a developer-defined instruction set injected at conversation start that shapes model behavior, output format, and constraints throughout the session. It is not visible to end users.
Query Fan-Out
AI breaks your one question into several smaller searches before writing the answer
AI
Simple version
When you ask AI 'what is the best VPN for streaming Netflix in Europe?' it does not just search that exact phrase. It breaks it into smaller pieces and searches each one separately — like 'best VPN 2026,' 'VPN Netflix streaming,' and 'VPN servers Europe.' Your brand needs to be findable from all of those angles, not just the main combined question.
Full definition
Query fan-out is the AI retrieval process of decomposing a complex user query into multiple simpler sub-queries, each searched independently to retrieve relevant context. The retrieved results are then synthesized into a single response. Brand GEO coverage must span multiple semantic angles — category, use case, audience, comparison, problem-solution — rather than optimizing for a single primary query.
Knowledge Graph / Wikipedia Signal
The web of facts AI trusts most — Wikipedia is the single most powerful signal in it
AI
Simple version
A knowledge graph is AI's version of an encyclopedia — a structured map of known facts about the world. Wikipedia is the most trusted source feeding into that map. Research of 231,347 AI responses found Wikipedia is the single strongest predictor of whether AI knows who you are. If a brand has a Wikipedia page, AI is dramatically more likely to cite it accurately and confidently.
Full definition
A knowledge graph is a structured database of entities and their relationships used by AI systems to ground factual claims. Wikipedia has a correlation coefficient of 0.810 with LLM visibility — the single strongest predictor in published GEO research. Reddit and G2 are the next most-cited domains. Building a knowledge graph presence means ensuring your brand is described accurately across Wikipedia, Reddit, review platforms, industry publications, and analyst reports.
tech Technical · 12 terms
Schema Markup / Structured Data
Hidden labels in your website code that tell AI exactly what everything means
Technical
Simple version
Your website has lots of text and numbers — but without labels, AI has to guess what everything means. Is '$49.99' a price or a ZIP code? Schema markup is hidden code that adds labels: 'this is the price,' 'this is the product name,' 'this is a customer review rating.' It is a standardized labeling language that both Google and AI understand instantly.
Full definition
Schema markup is structured data added to web pages using the Schema.org vocabulary to communicate page content in machine-readable format. JSON-LD is the recommended implementation — a script block embedded in page HTML defining entities, properties, and relationships. Product, Organization, Review, FAQPage, and BreadcrumbList schema types are highest-impact for GEO. Sites with complete schema markup are cited 30% more often in AI results.
Why it matters for your brand
Signal 4 in the CAIO diagnostic. The Schema Remediation Log in your brief itemizes every specific schema gap with prioritized fix instructions. Most Shopify stores have schema — but it is incomplete. The quality gap is the billable work.
JSON-LD
The specific code format used to write schema markup — the preferred method for AI
Technical
Simple version
JSON-LD is the specific format used to write schema markup. It is a block of code added to a web page that looks like a neat list of labeled facts: your brand name, what you sell, your rating, your address. Google recommends it, and AI crawlers love it because it is clean and unambiguous. Think of it as the official filing format for telling the internet exactly who you are.
Full definition
JSON-LD (JavaScript Object Notation for Linked Data) is a W3C standard method for encoding structured data as a script tag in HTML. It is separate from visible page content, easy to validate, and unambiguous for machine parsing. A JSON-LD block for a product page includes @type, name, description, price, brand, aggregateRating, and other Schema.org properties.
Schema Drift / Schema Remediation Log
When your schema becomes outdated as AI updates — and the prioritized list of fixes
Technical
Simple version
When AI models are updated, they sometimes change what labels they look for. If your schema does not keep up, it slowly becomes less useful — that slow decay is called schema drift. The Schema Remediation Log is the specific fix list your CAIO diagnostic produces — exactly which schema properties are missing, wrong, or outdated, in priority order.
Full definition
Schema drift is the degradation in schema effectiveness caused by model updates or evolving AI crawler specifications. The Schema Remediation Log (SRL) is a CAIO deliverable itemizing every specific schema gap with prioritized remediation instructions. One of the three primary outputs of the CAIO brief alongside the VAS and CPI.
llms.txt
A welcome note for AI crawlers — tells them exactly who you are and what you sell
Technical
Simple version
Imagine leaving a clear note on your front door that says: 'Hello AI, here is who we are, what we sell, and why customers love us.' That is what an llms.txt file does. It is a simple text file on your website written in plain language that gives AI crawlers a clean organized introduction to your brand. Shopify now auto-creates a basic version — but most auto-generated files are incomplete stubs.
Full definition
llms.txt is an emerging standard file placed at the website root (domain.com/llms.txt) providing AI crawlers with a structured brand summary — purpose, key pages, product categories, and important context. Shopify natively generates llms.txt for merchant stores, but quality audits consistently find auto-generated files are incomplete — missing brand context, product taxonomy, and positioning language.
robots.txt
The 'who can enter' sign on your website — accidentally blocking AI is the number 1 GEO mistake
Technical
Simple version
robots.txt is like a sign on your shop's entrance telling automated visitors which areas they are allowed in. For AI visibility, the critical question is whether your robots.txt is accidentally telling ChatGPT's search robot, or Perplexity's robot, to stay out — because that makes your site completely invisible to that AI platform.
Full definition
robots.txt is a plain text file at the website root specifying which automated crawlers are permitted or denied access. AI search bots (OAI-SearchBot, PerplexityBot, Claude-SearchBot) are completely distinct from AI training bots (GPTBot, CCBot) — blocking search bots destroys AI visibility while blocking training bots prevents content from entering model training datasets. The most common GEO mistake in 2026 is Cloudflare's Bot Fight Mode blocking all AI bots simultaneously.
agents.md
An instruction manual for AI shopping agents — how to navigate and buy on your store
Technical
Simple version
Similar to llms.txt, but specifically written for AI shopping agents. It tells them how to actually shop on your store — where to find products, how checkout works, what your return policy is. Think of it as an employee manual for AI robots that want to buy things on behalf of customers. Most stores do not have one yet.
Full definition
agents.md is an emerging convention for a machine-readable file providing structured operational guidance to autonomous AI shopping agents — product discovery pathways, checkout flow descriptions, policy information, and API endpoints for agentic interaction. Still in early adoption as of May 2026.
Prompt Caching
Saving AI's reading notes so it does not re-read the same instructions every time — saves 90%
Technical
Simple version
Every time you send instructions to AI, it re-reads them from scratch and you pay per word read. Prompt caching is like giving AI a bookmark. The first time it reads the instructions, it saves its notes. Next time, it just uses the notes instead of re-reading everything. This cuts costs by up to 90% on the repeated parts.
Full definition
Prompt caching is a Claude API feature that stores processed input tokens server-side for reuse. Cached tokens are billed at 10% of the standard input price on reads (90% savings) after a one-time write at 125% of normal cost. Cache duration is 5 minutes (ephemeral) or 1 hour. Highest-value for large static content reused across many API calls — like the CAIO system prompt which is identical across every audit.
Batch API / Batch Processing
Drop off a pile of work, come back later, pay half price — 50% off all AI processing
Technical
Simple version
Normally AI answers right away — real-time, like a phone call. Batch processing is like dropping off a pile of work overnight. You submit everything, walk away, and pick up the finished results later — usually within an hour, guaranteed within 24. The trade-off for waiting: exactly 50% off. No minimum size. The CAIO AI Visibility Index runs entirely on batch processing.
Full definition
The Batch API processes requests asynchronously at a flat 50% discount on all token prices — input and output — with no minimum volume threshold. Results available within 24 hours (typically under 1 hour). Stacked with prompt caching, total costs can drop to approximately one-tenth of the non-optimized baseline.
Input Tokens / Output Tokens / max_tokens
What you send to AI vs what it sends back — and why output costs 5× more
Technical
Simple version
When you use AI, you send it text (input tokens) and it sends text back (output tokens). They are billed separately — and output costs five times more than input. So keeping AI's answers focused and the right length matters a lot for cost control at scale. max_tokens is a setting that limits how long AI's response can be.
Full definition
Input tokens are the text tokens sent to the model. Output tokens are generated by the model and cost 5× the input rate across all Claude models — making output length the largest single cost lever. max_tokens is an API parameter capping the maximum output length per request. Setting max_tokens: 4096 on CAIO audit calls prevents runaway output generation while preserving full brief quality.
TTL — Time to Live
How long a cached item stays saved before the system forgets it and starts fresh
Technical
Simple version
TTL is how long something stays saved in a cache before it expires. If a cached prompt has a 5-minute TTL, the AI keeps those reading notes for 5 minutes. After that, the notes disappear and it has to re-read everything. For prompt caching to save money, you need to use the same cached content multiple times within the TTL window.
Full definition
TTL (Time to Live) is the duration a cached item remains valid before expiring. Claude's prompt cache offers two TTL options: 5-minute ephemeral cache (write at 1.25× input price, read at 0.10×) and 1-hour cache (write at 2.0× input price, read at 0.10×). Break-even requires enough cache reads within the TTL to offset the higher write cost.
CDN — Content Delivery Network
A global network that makes your website load fast everywhere in the world
Technical
Simple version
A CDN is a network of servers spread around the world. Instead of all visitors loading your website from a single computer in one location, a CDN serves your website from the server closest to each visitor. A visitor in Tokyo gets the site from a server in Tokyo. A visitor in London gets it from London. Cloudflare is the most widely used CDN — and the one caioinsight.ai uses.
Full definition
A Content Delivery Network is a distributed infrastructure of edge servers that cache and serve website content from locations proximate to end users, reducing latency. Cloudflare is the dominant CDN protecting 22% of all websites. Cloudflare Workers is Cloudflare's serverless compute layer — the hosting platform for caioinsight.ai.
Client-Side vs Server-Side Rendering
Whether your page content is built in the browser or on the server — AI can only see the server version
Technical
Simple version
Some websites are like a pre-built sandwich — the whole thing arrives fully assembled. That is server-side rendering. Other websites send a bag of ingredients that your browser assembles after it arrives. That is client-side rendering. AI crawlers do not have a browser to assemble ingredients — they only see what arrives pre-built. If your product content is assembled in the browser, AI sees an empty page.
Full definition
Server-side rendering (SSR) generates complete HTML on the server before sending to the browser — AI crawlers receive fully populated content. Client-side rendering (CSR) uses JavaScript to populate content in the browser — AI crawlers receive a nearly empty page. React, Vue, and headless CMS implementations commonly use CSR. Verifying SSR for product-critical pages is a standard CAIO diagnostic check.
seo SEO & Search · 9 terms
SERP — Search Engine Results Page
The Google list of blue links — increasingly being replaced by AI answers
SEO
Simple version
The SERP is the results page you see when you type something into Google — the list of blue links. Getting to page one of Google used to be the whole game. Now AI summaries appear above those links and answer the question directly. The overlap between brands ranking in Google's SERP and brands cited in AI answers has dropped from 70% to below 20% — they are becoming two completely separate visibility systems.
Full definition
A Search Engine Results Page is the page displayed in response to a search query. Gartner predicts a 25% drop in traditional search volume by 2026 as AI-generated answers displace SERP clicks. GEO and SEO are increasingly independent disciplines requiring separate optimization strategies and separate performance measurement.
Domain Authority / Backlink
Your website's reputation score — built by other trusted sites linking to you
SEO
Simple version
Domain authority is like a reputation score for your website. The more other trusted websites link to you, the higher your score. Those links are called backlinks. Getting the New York Times to link to you is like a celebrity endorsement — it tells both Google and AI that you are trustworthy and credible. Building domain authority takes time but creates a lasting advantage.
Full definition
Domain authority (DA) is a third-party metric predicting search ranking performance, based primarily on backlink quantity and quality from authoritative sources. High-quality backlinks signal topical expertise and entity credibility to both traditional search engines and AI citation systems. Brands with strong cross-domain mention profiles are more stably represented in LLM training data.
Topical Authority / E-E-A-T
Being the recognized expert on a subject — Google and AI both reward this heavily
SEO
Simple version
Topical authority means being the recognized expert on a topic. If your whole website is about one specific thing — like trail running gear — search engines and AI start to think of you as the go-to source for that topic. E-E-A-T stands for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness — Google's four-part test for whether a source deserves to be recommended.
Full definition
Topical authority is a domain's perceived expertise in a subject area, developed through comprehensive consistent content on a narrow topic cluster. E-E-A-T is Google's quality evaluation framework used to assess content quality for both traditional search ranking and AI Overview inclusion eligibility.
Subdomain vs Subdirectory
A separate house vs a room in your house — subdirectories are almost always better for GEO
SEO
Simple version
A subdomain is like a separate house on the same street — different address, different reputation (blog.yoursite.com). A subdirectory is like a room in the same house — same address, just a different door (yoursite.com/blog). For both SEO and AI visibility, keeping everything inside the same house is almost always better — authority from every room strengthens the whole house together.
Full definition
Subdomains are treated as separate entities by both search engines and AI systems — they do not automatically inherit parent domain authority, backlink equity, or entity recognition signals. Subdirectories inherit all root domain authority. For GEO specifically, subdomains fragment LLM entity recognition, split citation history, and reset AI coordinate presence to zero.
Sitemap / Crawl Budget
A directory of all your pages, and how many a crawler will visit per day
SEO
Simple version
A sitemap is a list of every page on your website, submitted to search engines so they know what to visit. Crawl budget is how many pages a crawler will read per day. For GEO, submitting your sitemap to Bing Webmaster Tools is a direct ChatGPT visibility action, since Bing powers ChatGPT's web search.
Full definition
An XML sitemap lists all indexable URLs on a site, submitted to search engines via Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools. Crawl budget is the number of pages a given crawler will fetch within a timeframe. AI crawlers maintain their own separate crawl budgets distinct from Googlebot.
Canonical Tag
Telling search engines which version of a page is the real one when duplicates exist
SEO
Simple version
Sometimes the same page on your website can be reached at multiple different web addresses. The canonical tag is a label that says 'this is the official version, ignore the others.' Without it, search engines and AI crawlers may split their attention between duplicates instead of focusing on the real page.
Full definition
A canonical tag (rel='canonical') is an HTML link element specifying the preferred URL for content when multiple URLs serve identical content. It consolidates link equity to the designated canonical URL and prevents duplicate content penalties.
Google Shopping / Product Feed
The database of your products that feeds both Google Shopping and ChatGPT carousels
